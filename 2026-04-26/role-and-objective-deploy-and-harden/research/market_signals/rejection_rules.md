# Rejection Rules

## Executive summary
Hard rejection rules were implemented in `src/trading_system/research/rejection.py`. A strategy is rejected if it fails any safety, robustness, data, execution, explainability, or operational condition.

## What was tested
Unit tests verify rejection for slippage-erased performance and too few symbols, and non-rejection when all hard evidence fields pass.

## Data used
Synthetic strategy evidence.

## Assumptions
Rejection is conservative. Passing rejection rules does not make a strategy eligible; it only avoids immediate rejection.

## Methodology
Rules evaluate slippage-adjusted return, symbol count, regime count, trade count, drawdown, parameter stability, outlier dependence, data quality, real-time availability, explainability, execution assumptions, bounded risk, kill-switch response, independent disablement, manual interpretation, stale-data behavior, turnover, spread/liquidity, and no-trade rules.

## Results
Implemented hard rules match the mandate.

## What passed
The evaluator produces explicit reasons.

## What failed
No real strategy evidence has been supplied to pass the rules.

## Rejected strategies
All current strategies are rejected for live-candidate status due to missing evidence.

## Strategies needing more paper/shadow validation
All strategies.

## Any restricted live-candidate strategies, if any
None.

## Blind spot check
Evidence quality matters. A strategy can pass a field with bad measurement unless inputs are generated by reproducible jobs.

## Operational risk notes
Do not override hard rejection reasons manually.
